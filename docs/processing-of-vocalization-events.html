Using Autonomous Recording Units to develop species distribution models for Amphibians in Alberta, Canada

3 Processing of vocalization events

The ARU data used to construct these SDM have come from the following ABMI surveys; Ecosystem Health sites (2015-2021); Adopt-a-Camera/ARU (2017); Citizen Science ARU (2016); Edge-Interior (2017); Northern Focal Areas (2019-2020); Southern Focal Areas (2020); Amphibian Monitoring (2020); and Operation Grassland (2015). Data collected through the Bioacoustic Units Wetlands surveys (2013-2019) was also incorporated. Due to the differences in how ARU data was collected between projects, and the time of deployment, recordings were standardized using the following procedure:

  • Only the first minute of each recording event was listened to.
  • Recordings with high levels of noises due to precipitation, wind conditions, or industrial and background activity were excluded permitting this metadata was available.
  • To replicate the conditions of a midnight survey, we filtered for recording events between 23:30-02:30 hours. We acknowledge that this survey interval may not be optimal for all vocalizing amphibians. Therefore, the calling phenology of each species will be investigated once sufficient data is available.
  • Only species that occurred at greater than 20 ARUs were maintained for analysis.
  • For all species with sufficient data, we assessed the seasonality window of calls by aggregating the percentage of detections across ARUs by Julian calendar day.
  • Local temperature during the recording event and Julian calendar day was used to account for the variation in detectability through the survey window.

After cleaning the data, there were 12330 unique recordings used for creating SDMs based on vegetation data (Boreal, Foothills, Canadian Shield, Rocky Mountain, and Parkland natural regions), and 4396 unique recordings used for creating SDMs based on soil data (Parkland and Grassland natural regions).
