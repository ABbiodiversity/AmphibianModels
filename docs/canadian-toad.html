Using Autonomous Recording Units to develop species distribution models for Amphibians in Alberta, Canada

Chapter 6 Canadian Toad

The Canadian Toad (Anaxyrus hemiophrys) is distributed throughout the eastern portion of Alberta, where it breeds in wetlands, slow-flowing streams, and ditches that are located in open areas, such as prairies and aspen parkland instead of forests (https://canadianherpetology.ca). This aligns with both our vegetation and soil based models where we predicted Canadian Toad to have low abundances in human footprint features. Interestingly, we predicted high abundances of Canadian Toad in young Pine stands (e.g., Jackpine, Lodgepole pine, Limber pine).

In our data set, Canadian Toad was moderately rare with detections at 16.1% of survey locations.

6.2 Vegetation Mdoels

We were able to create SDMs using both the vegetation (AUC = 0.76) and soil (AUC = 0.66) land cover with moderate to poor fit, respectively. The vegetation based model indicated that this species is predicted to have the highest abundance in shrubby swamps, shrubby fens, shrubby bog, and marsh native habitats in addition to high abundance in crops, rough pasture, tame pasture, cut-blocks, and urban industrial footprints. In addition, there was a strong positive effect of MWMT and a positive quadratic relationship with the proportion of surrounding water.

We were able to create SDMs using both the vegetation (AUC = 0.86) and soil (AUC = 0.83) land cover with good fit. The vegetation based model indicated that this species is predicted to have the highest abundance in young pine native habitats native habitats, and a low abundance in crops, rough pasture, and tame pasture footprints. In addition, there was a strong positive effect of MWMT and a weak negative effect with MCMT.

6.3 Soil Mdoels

Using the soil based models, we predicted that this species would have the highest abundance in rapid drain and thin break native habitats, and low abundance in crops, rough pasture, tame pasture, and urban industrial footprints. There was a positive quadratic relationship with MWMT in addition to a weak negative relationship with MAT. As we had good model fit for both the vegetation and soil based models, we created spatial predictions using both models.

The predicted relative abundance (bars) of Boreal Chorus Frog as a function of soil and human footprint types in the prairie region. Black vertical lines represent 90/% confidence intervals. The presence/absence of trees affects the presence and abundance of many species; therefore, separate figures are presented for treed and non-treed sites in the prairie region.

6.6 Forested Region

Local sector Predicted changes to species relative abundance inside areas that have been disturbed by each sector (human footprint type) compared to the habitat it replaced (modelled reference condition with no human footprint). Sector effect values less than 0% indicate habitat suitability is reduced (predicted related abundance is lower) compared to reference conditions, and values more than 0% indicate habitat suitability is improved (predicted relative abundance is higher) compared to reference conditions.

Regional sector Predicted change in the total regional population by industrial sector for species. This incorporates the area of the footprint, the native habitats where the footprint occurs, and the species response to a particular footprint. Regional population effect values less than 0% indicate a predicted decrease in the regional population due to a particular sector’s footprint, and values greater than 0% indicate a predicted increase.
